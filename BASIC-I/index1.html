The Brown Bear

About Brown Bears

The bear (Ursus arctos) is native to part of northen Eurasia and Nort America. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern

There are many subspecies within the brown bear species, including the atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

Species

  • Arctos
  • Collarus
  • Horribilis
  • Nelsono (extinct)

Features

Brown bears are not always completely brown. Some can be reddish or yellowish. They have very large, curved claws and huge paws. Male brown are often 30% larger than female brown bears. They can range from 5 feet to 9 feet from head to toe.
